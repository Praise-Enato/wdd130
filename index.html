Edo State | Nigeria

Nigeria is a vibrant and diverse West African country known for its rich cultural heritage, bustling cities, and beautiful landscapes. With over 200 million people, it is the most populous country in Africa, boasting a mix of ethnic groups like the Hausa, Yoruba, and Igbo. Its economy is one of the largest on the continent, driven by the oil and gas industry, as well as natural resources and agriculture. Nigeria's cultural scene is lively, with a strong emphasis on music, dance, and art, and it is home to the renowned Nollywood film industry. The country gained independence from British colonial rule in 1960 and has a complex history of various kingdoms and empires. English is the official language, but there are over 500 indigenous languages spoken. Nigeria's diverse wildlife and national parks make it a fascinating destination.
